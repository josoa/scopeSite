New NCAA rule allows football programs to use instant replay systems during games!

As of March 8, 2016, the NCAA Playing Rules Oversight Panel approved several new rules for the 2016 football season. The most notable of these rule changes being the authorization of electronic devices, such as Sport Scopeâ€™s Edge Replay, for coaching purposes in certain areas of stadiums.

Effective immediately, the use of electronic coaching devices, such as instant replay systems, are allowed to be used in the locker room and the press box. Coaches are still prohibited from using such devices on the sidelines and on the field, but allowing coaches to utilize replay systems at halftime and in the press box creates an enormous competitive advantage for every athletic program.

This rule change may also benefit Massachusetts and Texas high schools. Currently, these two states prohibit the use of instant replay during games for high school football programs. However, these two states historically follow the playing rules of the NCAA, which means they may choose to adopt these rules, like most other states, this upcoming season.

To learn more about this NCAA rule change, please visit their website.
